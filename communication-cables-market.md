<p><p>The communication cables market is categorized into three primary types: copper, aluminum, and aluminum alloy cables. Copper cables are widely used for their excellent conductivity and reliability, making them ideal for various communication applications. Aluminum cables are lighter and more cost-effective, offering good performance but lower conductivity than copper. Aluminum alloy cables combine the benefits of both metals, providing strength and reduced weight while maintaining decent conductivity. Each type serves specific needs in telecommunications, broadcasting, and data transmission industries.</p></p>
