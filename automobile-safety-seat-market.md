<p><p>The automobile safety seat market includes various types designed to protect children during travel. Infant seats are rear-facing and suitable for newborns and small infants. Convertible seats can switch between rear-facing and forward-facing positions, accommodating children as they grow. Booster seats elevate older children to ensure seat belts fit properly. Combination seats combine the features of both forward-facing harnesses and booster seats, providing extended use as a child ages. Each type targets specific age and weight categories for optimal safety.</p></p>
