<p><p>The combined hormonal contraceptive market encompasses three main types: pills, patches, and rings. Pills are oral contraceptives taken daily, providing a convenient option for users. Patches are adhesive strips applied to the skin, releasing hormones gradually into the bloodstream, with weekly change required. Rings are flexible devices inserted into the vagina, delivering hormones locally and replaced monthly. Each method offers varied benefits regarding adherence, convenience, and user preference, contributing to a diversified market catering to different needs and lifestyles.</p></p>
