<p><p>The automotive shock absorber rubber market is primarily divided into two types: natural rubber and chloroprene rubber. Natural rubber, derived from latex, offers excellent elasticity and resilience, making it ideal for absorbing shocks and vibrations in vehicles. Chloroprene rubber, on the other hand, is a synthetic alternative known for its resistance to wear, aging, and environmental conditions. Both types are crucial for enhancing vehicle performance and comfort, ensuring durability and safety during operation. Each type serves specific applications based on performance requirements.</p></p>
