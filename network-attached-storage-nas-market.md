<p><p>Network-Attached Storage (NAS) refers to a file-level storage solution connected to a network, allowing data access for multiple users and devices. The NAS market can be categorized into two types: scale-up and scale-out. Scale-up NAS involves adding more storage capacity to a single device, enhancing performance but with potential limitations on scalability. Scale-out NAS, on the other hand, expands by adding multiple storage devices, enabling greater flexibility and performance as data demands grow, making it ideal for large-scale environments.</p></p>
